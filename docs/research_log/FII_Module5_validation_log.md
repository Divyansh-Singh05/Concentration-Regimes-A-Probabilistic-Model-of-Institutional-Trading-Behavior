# FII Regime Detection — Module 5 Log: Economic Validation, Stage 1 (Price Panel & Forward Returns)
*Companion to `FII_Module3_validation_log.md`. Covers 2026-07-09. Scripts: `module5a_price_panel.py`, `module5b_forward_returns.py` (retired), `module5b1_ca_factors.py`, `module5b2_apply_adjustment.py`, `module5b2d_diagnose.py`, `module5b3_car_start.py` (pending).*

## 0. What Module 5 has to establish

Module 3 proved the labels are *statistically* stable (signatures, census, thresholds, effect sizes all replicate out-of-sample). It proved nothing *economic*. The economic claims, pre-registered since the framework was designed:

| Archetype | Economic story | Price prediction |
|---|---|---|
| SHARK_ACC | informed conviction buying | stock keeps rising after the episode (drift, no reversal) |
| SHARK_DIST | informed selling | stock keeps falling after the episode |
| HOSTAGE | forced fire-sale | price pushed below fair value → bounces back **after selling stops** (Coval-Stafford reversal) |
| ROBOT | passive/index flow | no prediction — ~zero abnormal returns |
| UNTAGGED | none | control, ~zero |

Testing this needs each stock's daily returns around each episode. The only price source is NSE bhavcopy (raw daily closes, 2011–2025, collected in Module 4). Module 5 Stage 1 = turn raw closes into **trustworthy returns**, then measure **forward returns after episodes**.

The central data hazard: bhavcopy prices are **unadjusted**. A 1:5 face-value split multiplies shares by 5 and divides price by 5 — the tape shows −80% that no investor experienced. Bonus issues likewise. Unfixed, a split inside a measurement window looks like a crash and poisons every test (especially HOSTAGE reversal). Most of Module 5 so far is about neutralizing this — and the log below is a chain of gates catching successive errors, exactly as designed.

## 1. Metric glossary (what every number in the outputs means)

- **ret_cc (close-to-close return)** — `close_t / close_{t-1} − 1` within one ISIN's price series. The raw daily return, corporate actions NOT removed.
- **Adjustment factor** — the multiple by which a corporate action divides the price on its **ex-date** (first day the share trades without the entitlement). FV split Rs.10→Re.1 ⇒ factor 10. Bonus a:b (a new shares per b held) ⇒ factor (a+b)/b, so 1:1 ⇒ 2, 1:2 ⇒ 1.5. Same-day split+bonus multiply (TITAN 2011: 10 × 2 = 20).
- **ret_adj (adjusted return)** — `(1 + ret_cc) × factor − 1` on ex-dates, `ret_cc` elsewhere. Undoes the mechanical drop, keeps the real market move (e.g. raw −94.7% on a ×20 day ⇒ adjusted +6.6%: the stock actually *rose* 6.6% that day).
- **Abnormal / market-adjusted return (ret_adj_mktadj)** — `ret_adj − NIFTY50 return` that day. Removes the market-wide move so we measure the *stock-specific* part. This is the crude "beta = 1" version; its known weakness is that small caps have beta ≠ 1 and drift vs a large-cap index — handled by reading results against a baseline row (below), with a per-stock-beta version as later robustness.
- **Episode** — a maximal run of consecutive same-archetype days within one stock (from `stockday_states_calibrated.parquet`). Median HOSTAGE episode is 2 days; ROBOT 11 days.
- **Anchor** — the day an event clock starts. **START** = first day of an episode (tests drift while the behavior unfolds). **END** = last day (tests what happens after the behavior stops — the correct anchor for fire-sale reversal, since Coval-Stafford reversal begins when the forced selling *ends*).
- **CAR_k (cumulative abnormal return)** — sum of daily abnormal returns over trading days t+1 … t+k after the anchor. Reported for k = 1, 5, 10, 20. Starts at **t+1**, not t, because the anchor day's return is mechanically correlated with the flow that defined the label (same-day price impact) — day 0 is reported separately as context, never as evidence.
- **bp (basis point)** — 0.01%. car20 = +45 bp means +0.45% cumulative abnormal return over 20 trading days (~1 month).
- **%pos** — share of events with car20 > 0. A mean can be dragged by a few extreme small caps; %pos and the **median** show whether the typical event agrees.
- **trunc% (truncation share)** — share of events where the stock stopped trading (delisting/suspension) inside the 20-day window. Those CARs are cut off at the last traded price and the event is **kept**. Dropping them would silently delete the worst HOSTAGE outcomes (fire-sale → bankruptcy) — survivorship bias exactly where it matters. Kept-but-truncated is still slightly optimistic (post-delisting value may be ~0), so trunc% is printed per archetype: if HOSTAGE truncation ≫ others, its CAR is flagged optimistic.
- **±50% clip** — daily abnormal returns are capped at ±50% before accumulating. Kills relist artifacts (a stock suspended at ₹0.15 relisting at ₹8.55 prints +5600% — real-ish but unusable); genuine circuit-limit days (±5–20%) are untouched.
- **Date-clustered bootstrap (N=1000)** — the significance machinery. Events are NOT independent: one crisis day launches dozens of HOSTAGE episodes that then share the same market path, so an iid t-test fakes precision. Instead we resample **calendar anchor-days** with replacement (each draw takes all events of that day), recompute the mean car20 each time, and read the 95% interval and a two-sided p from the resulting distribution. Clustering by day is the conservative, honest unit.
- **ALL_LABELED baseline row** — mean forward CAR over *every* labeled stock-day of that era. If the whole universe drifts +50 bp vs NIFTY (small-cap tilt in a bull era), every archetype inherits that shift; archetypes must be read **relative to this row**, not vs zero. (Motivated by the voided first run, where the TEST era showed +36…+52 bp for *everything including ROBOT* — a classic benchmark-mismatch level shift.)
- **Gate metrics** — Gate A/0 use the **median |return| on known split/bonus ex-days**: unadjusted data ⇒ ~0.49 (a factor-2 event halves the price ⇒ −50%; factor 2 is the modal event); properly adjusted ⇒ <0.05 (an ordinary trading day). Step-1 verification uses **obs_ratio = prev_close/close on the ex-day**, which should equal the parsed factor within ×/÷1.3 (band allows the same-day market move).

## 3. Standing method limitations (for the paper's caveats section)
1. Beta=1 market adjustment; baseline row mitigates, per-stock beta robustness pending.
2. Dividends not adjusted (small at daily scale; standard at this noise level).
3. Rights issues not adjusted (need issue price); 333 events, counted.
4. Truncated CARs value delisted stocks at last traded price (optimistic for HOSTAGE; trunc% printed so the bias is visible).
5. Return chains break at ISIN changes (splits mint new ISINs) — affected ex-days are null, not wrong; stitching via canonical-ISIN mapping is future work.
6. 2024–25 HOSTAGE prevalence remains coverage-confounded (Module 3 finding) — interpret late-TEST Hostage rows with that in mind.

## 3b. Join-attrition diagnostic (5D) — crosswalk hypothesis REJECTED
Concern raised: the ~9.6% G2 join miss might be ISIN-churn (splits/restructurings mint new ISINs) disproportionately blinding HOSTAGE, needing a canonical↔historical crosswalk (the `isin_lookup.parquet` / `isin_mapping_final.csv` tables). `module5d_attrition_diagnostic.py` decomposed the miss:
- **Cause A** (model cisin absent from the tape entirely → crosswalk-fixable): **0.5%** of unmatched.
- **Cause B** (cisin IS in the tape, just no price on that date → genuine non-trading): **99.5%**.
A crosswalk would recover ~0.05% of all rows ⇒ **not built.** Cause B is structural: NSDL FII data is exchange-agnostic, our panel is NSE-only, so BSE-primary days / pre-listing / post-delisting / suspension days yield an FII trade with no NSE close — correctly dropped (no price ⇒ no return).
- Distress names (RCOM, DHFL, HDIL, YESBANK, …) match 83.9% vs 90.4% overall — real but also Cause B (distressed stocks are suspended/halted more). Not a mapping defect. Residual survivorship risk lives in the forward-CAR **window** (halt-to-zero mid-window), handled by CAR truncation (trunc% reported per archetype in 5B-3/4), not the anchor join.
- **START vs END anchor match rates are identical** for HOSTAGE (95.9/95.9 TEST, 88.8/88.8 TRAIN) and within ~0.5pt for all archetypes ⇒ 5B-4's reversal anchors are NOT differentially lost. The feared mechanism does not occur.
Existing ISIN tables (`isin_lookup.parquet` 752 rows canonical↔old, `isin_mapping_final.csv` 1375, `restructure_recovered.csv` 48, `isin_master_clean.parquet` 4639 with first/last_trade) remain available if a future need arises, but are not needed here.
